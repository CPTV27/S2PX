The Problem We Solve
The scan-to-BIM industry suffers from an accountability crisis. Buyers receive deliverables labeled "LoD 300" with no validation evidence, unclear accuracy standards, mystery coordinates, and no defined support. Six common failures S2P exists to eliminate:
Failure
What Happens
Undefined LoD
"Detailed model" without specifying elements, geometry level, or validation method

"High accuracy" claims without registration reports, RMS residuals, or overlap metrics
Mystery Coordinates

Silent Voids
Point clouds with undocumented gaps; clients assume complete coverage
No Acceptance Criteria
No defined process for determining whether deliverables meet requirements
Absent Support
"Not our responsibility" or "additional fee" when issues arise after delivery
These failures are systemic ‚Äî they exist because the industry lacks accountability frameworks. S2P's 12-Point Assurance Standard explicitly addresses every one.

What We Promise vs What We Don't
We promise:
- Point cloud accuracy validated to LoA-40 with registration reports proving it
- BIM model accuracy at LoA-30 for elements within contracted scope
- LoD specifications documented and met for each discipline
- Coverage limitations documented with reasons (not silent voids)
- Assumptions explicitly identified (not hidden)
- QA Packet with validation evidence accompanying every deliverable
- Unlimited support for documented scope
We don't promise:
- Concealed conditions (inside walls, above inaccessible ceilings, below grade)
- Elements below MEP sizing thresholds unless specifically contracted
- Material specifications or conditions beyond visible geometry
- Areas not scanned due to access restrictions (documented in QA Packet)
- Perfect orthogonal geometry (real buildings aren't orthogonal)
- Structural capacity, code compliance, or engineering certifications
