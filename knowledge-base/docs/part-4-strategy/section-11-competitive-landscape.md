Market Position Summary
S2P operates in the Scan-to-BIM services market: pure-play scan-to-BIM firms, surveying companies with BIM capabilities, large AEC consultancies offering scanning as add-on, and offshore modeling shops. Over 100 direct competitors.

Market Tells
- Competitors sell nouns, not outcomes. Out-position with risk transfer + design time saved.
- Almost no LoD/LoA in public = fear of accountability. Publish standards and win trust.
- Risk unaddressed. As-Built Assurance is wide open to own.
- Sustainability untapped. Tie scanning to adaptive reuse/carbon.
- Procurement language missing. No vendor-onboarding or portfolio standardization talk.
- Education vacuum. Thought leadership ‚Üí pipeline for the taking.
- Visual proof underused. Viewer + QA packet front and center is novel.
- "Nationwide" is their default flex. Counter with SLA'd mobilization + guaranteed outcomes.

Competitive Counter-Moves by Archetype
- Nationwide Scanners (PPM/GPRS/Arrival): Lead with Guarantee + QA packet + price-match at equal spec. Reframe "nationwide" as consistency risk without standards.
- Big AEC Divisions (AECOM/Stantec/Gensler): Position specialist focus + spec-first program; offer to standardize vendors under S2P acceptance criteria.
- Legacy Pioneers (Direct Dimensions/Existing Conditions): Counter "experience" with contractual outcomes + viewer-led proof.
- Low-Cost Boutiques: Disarm with price-match at equal spec and risk-cost story (change orders vs. fee).

## Swot
- Strengths: Published LoA/LoD; accuracy & turnaround guarantees; 1-week mobilization / 2-5 week modeling; client-template delivery; HBIM expertise; transparent TKB; unlimited in-scope revisions; 7.5-year production team.
- Weaknesses: Newer brand vs. incumbents with 10K+ buildings; regional hubs (Troy/NYC core); smaller public case library (growing); key-person dependency (Owen sole closer); 2022-vintage website.
- Opportunities: Thought-leadership vacuum (CEUs, guides, webinars); "Design Freedom" narrative; portfolio standardization for enterprise; sustainability/adaptive-reuse story; partner co-marketing; spec-first competitive moat; education white space.
- Threats: Commoditization/price pressure ("scanner-in-a-van" bids); in-house capture (GCs/megafirms buying scanners); AI-assisted modeling automation; big-firm bundling (integrated AEC cross-sell); macro construction cycles; renovation slowdowns.
