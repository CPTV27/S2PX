### 9.A â€” HBIM Characteristics (LoD 350+)
- Non-orthogonal geometry: Historic buildings rarely conform to modern orthogonal construction. HBIM captures irregularities rather than forcing orthogonal approximations.
- Custom family creation: Standard Revit families cannot represent historic molding profiles, ornamental metalwork, or period-specific features.
- Material documentation: Beyond geometry â€” documenting existing materials, conditions, deterioration patterns for conservation planning.
- Annotation integration: HBIM models integrate with HABS documentation standards and preservation-specific annotation requirements.
Common HBIM Applications:
- Secretary of Interior Standards compliance documentation
- Historic Tax Credit applications requiring measured drawings
- Adaptive reuse projects preserving character-defining features
- Cultural heritage digitization for archival purposes
- Grant compliance (NPS, SHPO, private foundation requirements)
