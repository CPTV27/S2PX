Pre-Scan Workflow
Before any scanning, Scan2Plan conducts four preparation activities:
1. Digital Site Survey. Google Earth, Zillow, and any client-supplied documentation (floor plans, site photos, prior surveys) are used to evaluate site access, entrances, parking, and scanning areas. Existing drawings help orient field work and validate coverage â€” helpful but not required.
2. Client Alignment & Scope Confirmation. Exact scan areas confirmed (interior-only, exterior-only, partial). Site access, security clearances, and escort requirements verified. Scope (LoD, LoA, deliverables, platform) re-confirmed before contract finalization.
3. Scheduling & Travel Logistics. Site visits scheduled via Airtable job tracking. Scan technicians assigned, travel logistics planned, lodging arranged if applicable. Typical mobilization: 1 week from contract approval.
4. Pre-Scan Equipment Check. Scanner calibration verified (Trimble X7, NavVis VLX2). PPE compliance confirmed (hard hats, safety vests, steel-toe boots, respirators, gloves as required).

What Happens On Site
Scanning is quiet and non-disruptive. The Trimble X7 emits an invisible laser and captures a full-dome scan in 3-4 minutes per position. Technicians move through the space methodically, typically spacing positions 20-25 feet apart to achieve 65-70% overlap between scans.
Scale reference: A typical 50,000 SF building requires 60-100 scan positions. Occupied buildings can be scanned during normal operations. Active construction sites require appropriate PPE and safety coordination.
White Glove Protocol: Technicians do not touch, move, or interact with client property unless explicitly required and pre-approved.
On-site review: Before leaving, technicians review captured scans to confirm all contracted areas have been covered. Field registration is validated on-site (Trimble X7 advantage â€” real-time cloud-to-cloud verification).

Revision Policy
Unlimited revisions within contracted scope. No extra fees. Applies to all deliverable types (BIM, CAD, point cloud, PDF).
Included in unlimited revisions:
- Corrections to existing elements (walls, doors, windows, beams) where errors are found
- File format changes (e.g., DWG instead of DXF)
- Template adjustments (title blocks, layer naming, Revit family names)
- Refinements based on client design review feedback
Requires change order:
- Adding areas not in original scope
- Adding disciplines (e.g., MEPF after architecture-only contract)
- Upgrading LoD (e.g., 300 to 350+)
- Re-scanning changed conditions
Revision turnaround:
- Small (wall alignment, template update): 1-3 business days
- Large (floor rework, LoD upgrade within scope): 1-2 weeks
- File format conversion: 1 business day
How to request: Email assigned Project Manager with specific request. Acknowledgment within 24 hours. Estimated timeline provided at acknowledgment. Changes verified by QC before re-delivery.

Post-Delivery Support
Support does not expire. No additional charge for scope-related questions. (See Section 1: Lifetime Support guarantee.)
What's included:

- Technical guidance (importing point clouds into Revit, file format compatibility, measurement tools in Trimble ScanExplorer)
- Corrections within contracted LoD/LoA
- Coordinate system clarification
- File format re-export
File retention: Indefinite. Google Drive and tape backup. No time limits, no storage fees. Clients who lose access can request file re-share â€” typically fulfilled within 1-3 business days.
How to request support: Email Project Manager or info@scan2plan.io. Acknowledgment within 24 hours. Small support requests resolved in 1-3 business days. Revision requests follow turnaround schedule above.
